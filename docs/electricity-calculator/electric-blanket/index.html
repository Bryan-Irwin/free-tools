Electric Blanket Tips

  • Use it to pre-heat your bed: Turn on the electric blanket 15-30 minutes before bedtime, then turn it off or to a low setting when you get in.
  • Lower your thermostat at night: An electric blanket uses far less energy than heating your entire home. Drop the thermostat a few degrees and use the blanket instead.
  • Choose one with an auto-shutoff: Blankets with automatic shutoff timers prevent running all night and reduce energy waste and safety risks.
  • Use a lower heat setting: Most electric blankets use significantly less wattage on low or medium settings compared to the maximum rating.
  • Layer with regular blankets: Place the electric blanket under a comforter or duvet to trap heat, allowing you to use a lower setting.
  • Inspect regularly: Check for worn wires, scorch marks, or damage. Replace old blankets to ensure safety and efficiency.
