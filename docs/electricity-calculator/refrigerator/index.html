Refrigerator Efficiency Tips

  • Set the right temperature: Keep your fridge at 37-40°F (3-4°C) and the freezer at 0°F (-18°C). Colder settings waste energy without improving food safety.
  • Keep coils clean: Dust and pet hair on condenser coils force the compressor to work harder. Clean them every 6-12 months.
  • Check door seals: Worn or loose gaskets let cold air escape. Test by closing the door on a piece of paper; if it slides out easily, replace the seal.
  • Keep it full but not packed: A well-stocked fridge retains cold better than an empty one, but overpacking blocks air circulation.
  • Let hot food cool first: Placing hot food directly in the fridge raises the internal temperature and forces the compressor to work harder.
  • Locate away from heat sources: Keep your fridge away from the oven, dishwasher, and direct sunlight to reduce the cooling load.
